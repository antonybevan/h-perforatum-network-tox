\section{Methods}

\subsection{Network data}

The protein--protein interaction network was obtained from STRING v12.0. 
Interactions with combined confidence score $\geq$ 900 were retained. 
Nodes were restricted to genes with liver expression $\geq$ 1 TPM (GTEx v8 median). 
The largest connected component (LCC) was extracted. 
Final network: 12,847 nodes, 89,432 edges.

\subsection{Compound targets}

Drug--target interactions were obtained from STITCH v5.0 (confidence $\geq$ 0.7) and ChEMBL v31 (activity $\leq$ 10 $\mu$M). 
Targets were mapped to gene symbols via UniProt. 
Targets absent from the liver LCC were excluded. 
Hyperforin: 10 targets. Quercetin: 62 targets.

\subsection{DILI genes}

Drug-induced liver injury (DILI) genes were obtained from DILIrank. 
Genes absent from the liver LCC were excluded. 
Final set: 82 DILI genes.

\subsection{Shortest-path proximity (descriptive)}

Shortest-path distance was computed between all compound target--DILI gene pairs. 
Mean distance was calculated. 
This metric is descriptive; it does not test functional influence. 
Null distribution: 1,000 degree-matched random target sets. 
Z-score: $(d_{\text{observed}} - \mu_{\text{null}}) / \sigma_{\text{null}}$. 
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Random walk with restart (inferential)}

Influence was quantified using random walk with restart. 
Restart probability: $\alpha = 0.15$ (per Guney et al., 2016). 
Restart vector: uniform over compound targets. 
Convergence tolerance: $10^{-6}$. 
Maximum iterations: 100. 
Total DILI influence: $\sum_{d \in D} p_d$.

Statistical testing: 1,000 degree-matched permutations. 
Degree matching: targets sampled from nodes with degree within 25\% of original targets. 
Z-score: $(I_{\text{observed}} - \mu_{\text{null}}) / \sigma_{\text{null}}$. 
$P$-value: one-tailed (greater). 
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Expression-weighted RWR (constraining)}

Expression-weighted RWR tests whether influence persists under tissue-specific constraint. 
Adjacency matrix modified: $A'_{ij} = A_{ij} \times \log_2(\text{TPM}_i + 1)$. 
Column normalization applied. 
All other parameters identical to standard RWR.

Statistical testing: 1,000 degree-matched permutations. 
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Per-target normalized influence (descriptive)}

PTNI = total DILI influence / target count. 
This is a derived ratio for visualization. 
No separate permutation test was performed.

\subsection{Bootstrap sensitivity analysis}

100 random 10-target subsets were sampled from Quercetin's 62-target pool. 
Each subset was scored by standard RWR. 
Distribution compared to Hyperforin's observed influence. 
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Chemical similarity control}

Morgan fingerprints (radius = 2, 2048 bits) were computed using RDKit v2023.03. 
Maximum Tanimoto similarity was computed against DILIrank reference drugs (542 DILI+, 365 DILI$-$). 
Analog threshold: 0.4.

\subsection{Multiple testing correction}

Benjamini--Hochberg FDR correction was applied within each network threshold. 
$P$-values at the permutation floor are reported as $< 10^{-16}$.
