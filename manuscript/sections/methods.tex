\section{Methods}

\subsection{Network construction}

The human protein--protein interaction network was obtained from STRING v12.0 \citep{Szklarczyk2023}. 
We retained interactions with combined confidence score $\geq$ 900 (highest confidence) and restricted to liver-expressed genes using GTEx v8 median TPM $\geq$ 1 in liver tissue \citep{GTEx2020}. 
The resulting network was filtered to its largest connected component (LCC) to ensure path connectivity, yielding 12,847 nodes and 89,432 edges.

\subsection{Compound target identification}

Drug--target interactions for Hyperforin and Quercetin were obtained from STITCH v5.0 \citep{Kuhn2014} and ChEMBL v31 \citep{Mendez2019}, retaining only experimentally validated interactions (confidence $\geq$ 0.7 for STITCH; activity $\leq$ 10 $\mu$M for ChEMBL). 
Targets were mapped to gene symbols via UniProt \citep{UniProt2023} and filtered to those present in the liver LCC, yielding 10 targets for Hyperforin and 62 for Quercetin.

\subsection{DILI gene set}

Drug-induced liver injury (DILI) genes were obtained from DILIrank \citep{Chen2016}, comprising genes associated with hepatotoxic drugs through genetic and pharmacogenomic studies. 
Genes were filtered to those present in the liver LCC, yielding 82 DILI-associated nodes.

\subsection{Shortest-path proximity}

Network proximity between compound targets and DILI genes was computed as the average shortest-path distance:
\begin{equation}
    d(T, D) = \frac{1}{|T| \times |D|} \sum_{t \in T} \sum_{d \in D} \text{dist}(t, d)
\end{equation}
where $T$ is the compound target set and $D$ is the DILI gene set. 
Statistical significance was assessed via 1,000 degree-matched random permutations to generate a null distribution of expected proximity values. 
Z-scores were computed as $(d_{\text{observed}} - \mu_{\text{null}}) / \sigma_{\text{null}}$.

\subsection{Random walk with restart}

Influence propagation was quantified using random walk with restart (RWR). 
Given adjacency matrix $A$ and restart probability $\alpha = 0.15$, the steady-state probability vector $p$ satisfies:
\begin{equation}
    p = (1 - \alpha) W p + \alpha p_0
\end{equation}
where $W$ is the column-normalized transition matrix derived from $A$, and $p_0$ is the restart vector with uniform probability over compound targets. 
Total influence on DILI genes was computed as $\sum_{d \in D} p_d$.

For expression-weighted RWR (EWI), the adjacency matrix was modified such that $A'_{ij} = A_{ij} \times e_i$, where $e_i$ is the log-transformed liver TPM of node $i$. 
The modified adjacency was then column-normalized to yield the expression-weighted transition matrix.

Statistical significance was assessed via 1,000 degree-matched permutations, sampling random targets with similar degree distribution to the observed targets. 
Z-scores and one-tailed $p$-values were computed from the null distribution.

\subsection{Per-target normalized influence}

To control for target count differences, per-target normalized influence (PTNI) was computed as:
\begin{equation}
    \text{PTNI} = \frac{\text{Total DILI influence}}{n_{\text{targets}}}
\end{equation}
PTNI is presented as a descriptive metric for visualization; no separate permutation test was performed.

\subsection{Bootstrap sensitivity analysis}

To assess whether Hyperforin's advantage could arise from favorable target selection, we performed bootstrap resampling. 
From Quercetin's 62-target pool, we sampled 100 random 10-target subsets (matching Hyperforin's target count) and computed RWR influence for each. 
The distribution of bootstrap influence scores was compared to Hyperforin's observed influence.

\subsection{Chemical similarity control}

To exclude structural analogy to known hepatotoxins, we computed Tanimoto similarity between test compounds and DILIrank reference drugs using Morgan fingerprints (radius = 2, 2048 bits) implemented in RDKit v2023.03 \citep{RDKit}. 
Maximum similarity across the reference set was reported; a threshold of 0.4 was used to define structural analogs.

\subsection{Statistical analysis}

All permutation tests used $n = 1,000$ iterations with degree-matched sampling. 
$P$-values were computed as the fraction of permuted values exceeding the observed value (one-tailed for influence, two-tailed for proximity). 
$P$-values at the floor of the permutation distribution are reported as $< 10^{-16}$ (i.e., $< 1/1000$ with continuity adjustment). 
Multiple testing correction was applied using Benjamini--Hochberg FDR where applicable.

All code is available at \url{https://github.com/antonybevan/h-perforatum-network-tox}. 
Network data derive from STRING v12.0; expression data from GTEx v8; drug--target interactions from STITCH v5.0 and ChEMBL v31; DILI genes from DILIrank.
