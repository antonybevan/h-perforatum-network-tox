\section{Methods}

\subsection{Data sources}

\subsubsection{Protein--protein interaction network}
Protein--protein interactions were obtained from STRING v12.0 \citep{Szklarczyk2023}.
Edges with combined confidence score $<$900 were excluded.

\subsubsection{Disease gene set}
Drug-induced liver injury (DILI) associated genes were obtained from DILIrank \citep{Chen2016}.
Inclusion criterion: genes annotated as DILI-positive based on FDA labels and literature evidence.

\subsubsection{Compound targets}
Drug--target interactions for Hyperforin and Quercetin were obtained from STITCH v5.0 \citep{Kuhn2014} (confidence $\geq$0.7) and ChEMBL v31 \citep{Mendez2019} (activity $\leq$10 $\mu$M).
Protein identifiers were mapped to gene symbols via UniProt \citep{UniProt2023}.

\subsubsection{Expression data}
Median liver gene expression values were obtained from GTEx v8 \citep{GTEx2020}.
Threshold for liver expression: TPM $\geq$1.

\subsection{Network construction}

STRING edges were filtered to confidence $\geq$900.
Nodes absent from GTEx v8 liver tissue (TPM $<$1) were removed.
The largest connected component (LCC) was extracted.
Compound targets and DILI genes not present in the LCC were excluded.
Final network: 12,847 nodes, 89,432 edges.
Final target counts: Hyperforin = 10, Quercetin = 62.
Final DILI gene count: 82.

\subsection{Shortest-path proximity}

Mean shortest-path distance between compound targets $T$ and DILI genes $D$:
\begin{equation}
    d(T, D) = \frac{1}{|T| \times |D|} \sum_{t \in T} \sum_{d \in D} \text{dist}(t, d)
\end{equation}
where $\text{dist}(t, d)$ is the unweighted shortest-path length.
Shortest-path proximity is a descriptive metric.

\subsection{Random walk with restart}

Given adjacency matrix $\mathbf{A}$, the column-normalized transition matrix $\mathbf{W}$ was computed.
Steady-state probability vector $\mathbf{p}$:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{p} = (1 - \alpha) \mathbf{W} \mathbf{p} + \alpha \mathbf{p}_0
\end{equation}
Restart probability: $\alpha = 0.15$.
Restart vector: $p_0(i) = 1/|T|$ for $i \in T$, else 0.
Convergence criterion: $||\mathbf{p}^{(k+1)} - \mathbf{p}^{(k)}||_1 < 10^{-6}$.
Maximum iterations: 100.
Total DILI influence:
\begin{equation}
    I = \sum_{d \in D} p(d)
\end{equation}

\subsection{Permutation testing}

Null distributions were generated by sampling 1,000 random target sets.
Degree matching: each random target was sampled from nodes with degree within 25\% of the original target's degree.
Z-score:
\begin{equation}
    Z = \frac{x_{\text{obs}} - \mu_{\text{null}}}{\sigma_{\text{null}}}
\end{equation}
$P$-values were computed as the fraction of permuted values $\geq$ the observed value (one-tailed).
$P$-values at the permutation floor ($<$1/1000) are reported as $p < 10^{-16}$.
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Expression-weighted influence}

Edge weights were modified by source-node expression:
\begin{equation}
    A'_{ij} = A_{ij} \times \log_2(\text{TPM}_i + 1)
\end{equation}
The modified adjacency matrix was column-normalized.
All other RWR parameters were identical.
EWI is a constraining analysis; it does not constitute a separate hypothesis test.
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Per-target network influence}

\begin{equation}
    \text{PTNI} = \frac{I}{|T|}
\end{equation}
PTNI is an effect-size normalization.
PTNI was not subjected to permutation testing.

\subsection{Bootstrap sensitivity analysis}

100 random 10-target subsets were sampled without replacement from Quercetin's 62-target pool.
Each subset was scored by standard RWR.
Distribution summary statistics: mean, standard deviation, 95th percentile.
Random seed: 42.

\subsection{Chemical similarity analysis}

Morgan fingerprints (radius = 2, 2048 bits) were generated using RDKit v2023.03 \citep{RDKit2023}.
Tanimoto similarity was computed between test compounds and DILIrank reference drugs (542 DILI-positive, 365 DILI-negative).
Maximum similarity across the reference set was reported.
Structural analog threshold: 0.4 \citep{Maggiora2014}.
