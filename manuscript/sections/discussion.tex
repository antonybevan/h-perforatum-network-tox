\section{Discussion}

Hyperforin, not Quercetin, drives the hepatotoxicity signal from \textit{Hypericum perforatum}.
The evidence is convergent: higher influence despite fewer targets, 22-fold greater per-target efficiency, persistence under expression weighting, and exclusion of target-count and structural-analog confounders.

\subsection{The proximity paradox}

Proximity suggested Quercetin as the greater concern \citep{Menche2015}.
Random walk with restart reversed the ranking.
Proximity counts paths; influence weights them by topology \citep{Kohler2008}.
The rank reversal demonstrates these are distinct properties.

\subsection{Mechanistic basis}

Hyperforin activates PXR, inducing CYP3A4, CYP2C9, and ABC transporters \citep{Moore2000, Watkins2001}.
These targets occupy topologically central positions in hepatic drug metabolism networks \citep{Klaassen2010}.
Quercetin's 62 targets disperse across flavonoid biosynthesis, signaling, and antioxidant pathways \citep{Boots2008}.
The 22-fold PTNI difference reflects focused versus scattered target profiles.

\subsection{Expression constraint}

Expression weighting narrows but does not reverse the gap.
Quercetin gains from high-expression targets (CFB); Hyperforin's topological advantage persists.
The constraint was applied; the ranking held.

\subsection{Implications}

Target count is a poor proxy for risk \citep{Heinrich2020}.
Few strategically positioned targets may pose greater liability than many peripheral ones.
These data support prioritizing Hyperforin content in safety-oriented quality control of \textit{H.\ perforatum} preparations \citep{Bauer2004}.

\subsection{Limitations}

Drug--target databases may be incomplete.
STRING aggregates across conditions; liver-specific networks might differ.
PTNI is descriptive, not formally tested.
Network predictions require experimental validation.
Extension to other constituents would strengthen generalizability.

\subsection{Conclusions}

Mechanistic specificity, not target promiscuity, drives Hyperforin's hepatotoxicity signal.
The combination of permutation-based inference, expression weighting, and control analyses provides a framework applicable to other complex botanicals.
