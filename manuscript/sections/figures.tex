(\textbf{A}) Target count in the liver-expressed largest connected component.

\begin{figure}[ht]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{figures/fig2_dumbbell.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Proximity does not predict influence: the rank reversal.}
Dumbbell plot showing the dissociation between shortest-path proximity (left) and random walk influence (right).
Quercetin exhibits closer proximity but weaker influence; Hyperforin exhibits greater influence despite more distant topology.
Hyperforin: proximity Z = $-$2.6, influence Z = +10.3 ($p < 10^{-16}$).
Quercetin: proximity Z = $-$5.4, influence Z = +4.4 ($p < 10^{-6}$).
Influence quantified by random walk with restart (RWR; $\alpha = 0.15$).
$n = 1{,}000$ degree-matched permutations per compound.}
\label{fig:fig2}
\end{figure}

\caption{\textbf{Expression weighting attenuates but does not reverse the advantage.}
Waterfall decomposition of Z-score changes under expression-weighted influence (EWI).
Initial differential: $\Delta$Z = +5.8 (RWR).
Hyperforin change: $-$1.2 (attenuation of CYP2C9, CYP3A4 high-expression advantage).
Quercetin change: +1.1 (gain from CFB at 1{,}115 TPM).
Residual differential: $\Delta$Z = +3.5.
Both compounds remain significant under EWI: Hyperforin Z = +9.1 ($p < 10^{-16}$); Quercetin Z = +5.6 ($p < 10^{-8}$).
Expression weighting from GTEx v8 liver tissue.}
\label{fig:fig3}
\end{figure}

\caption{\textbf{Per-target network influence (PTNI) reveals efficiency disparity.}
Phase plot of total influence versus target count.
Diagonal lines represent iso-efficiency contours (PTNI = constant).
Hyperforin occupies a higher efficiency region despite fewer targets.
PTNI values: Hyperforin = 0.0114 (RWR), 0.0133 (EWI); Quercetin = 0.0005 (RWR), 0.0008 (EWI).
Fold difference: 22$\times$ (RWR), 17$\times$ (EWI).
PTNI is an effect-size normalization (influence / targets); no independent permutation test was performed.}
\label{fig:fig4}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[ht]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{figures/fig5_bootstrap.pdf}
\caption{\textbf{Bootstrap sensitivity analysis excludes target-count confounding.}
Density distribution of RWR influence scores from 100 random 10-target samples drawn from Quercetin's 62-target pool.
Shaded region: 95\% confidence interval (0.016--0.054).
Vertical line: Hyperforin observed influence (0.114).
Hyperforin exceeds the entire bootstrap distribution (3.7$\times$ fold vs.\ mean).
This confirms that Hyperforin's advantage is not attributable to favorable target count.
Bootstrap is a robustness control; it does not provide independent statistical evidence.}
\label{fig:fig5}
\end{figure}
